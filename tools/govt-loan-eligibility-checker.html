Govt Loan Eligibility Checker

Two of India's biggest non-collateral business loan schemes — PM Vishwakarma (artisans, 18 traditional trades) and PM Mudra (Shishu / Kishore / Tarun) — have very different eligibility rules. Answer 3 questions and see which scheme fits, with the loan range and documents you'll need.

PM Mudra slabs

  • Shishu — up to ₹50,000. For pre-revenue or just-starting businesses.
  • Kishore — ₹50,001 to ₹5 lakh. For businesses up & running, needing scale capital.
  • Tarun — ₹5,00,001 to ₹10 lakh. For established businesses ready to expand. Tarun Plus (up to ₹20 lakh) is also available since FY 2024-25 for borrowers who have repaid an earlier Tarun loan on time.

FAQ

Can I apply for both schemes?

No, not simultaneously. PM Vishwakarma beneficiaries are not eligible for PM Mudra during the active loan period. Pick whichever fits — Vishwakarma is cheaper (5% rate) but only for the 18 listed trades and capped at ₹3L.

Do I need an Udyam / Udyog Aadhaar?

For Mudra Tarun (₹5L+), most banks expect Udyam registration. Shishu and Kishore are usually fine without it. Vishwakarma uses its own portal-based registration; you don't need Udyam separately.

How long does Vishwakarma processing take?

Three-step process: portal registration → Gram Panchayat / ULB verification → district committee approval. Usually 30-45 days end-to-end. Skill training (5-7 days) can run in parallel and unlocks the ₹15K toolkit voucher.

What if my Mudra application is rejected?

Mudra has no central eligibility test — each bank decides. If one rejects, try another (PSU banks have higher Mudra disbursal targets than private banks). NBFC-MFIs are usually most accessible for Shishu loans.

Is the interest tax-deductible?

Yes — interest paid on either loan is a business expense, fully deductible from your business income. Use our Income Tax Calculator for the bottom-line impact.

Note: Eligibility rules can change with each annual budget. Always check the official portal before applying. Other schemes worth knowing: Stand-Up India (SC/ST + women, ₹10L–₹1Cr), Stree Shakti (women entrepreneurs, SBI), Credit Guarantee Fund Trust for MSE (CGTMSE) — also collateral-free.
